The Diets of Ancient Athletes

Another important topic to discuss is the diets of the ancient Greek athletes. The diet of an athlete is quite potentially the most important part of being an athlete, at least in the modern era. This makes it important to discuss the ideology of nutrition in the ancient world. According to Jan Bažant, the ancient Greek athletes were absolute gluttons: “As long as the 5th century B.C. the Greek athlete was notorious for being a slave to his jaw and his belly” (Bažant). This is important to note, as it shows that these athletes probably ate much more than they should have. It is also important to know the diets of the average ancient Greek person. Because Greece has direct access to the sea, their diet generally consisted of seafood (Jensen). This can be shown by marine life on pottery, such as squid and octopi on pots (Jensen). This shows that even while the athletes were gluttons, they were still probably eating high-protein meals. However, the fact that the ancient athletes were considered to be “slaves to their jaws and their bellies” (Bažant) shows how little the people back then understood about nutrition. According to Jan Bažant, the diets of the ancient Greeks did not line up with The American Association for Health, Physical Education and Recreation: “Ancient Greek sportsmen really did not comply with the principles propagated by The American Association for Health, Physical Education and Recreation” (Bažant). This proves even further that the ancient Greeks had no clue what they were doing in regard to their diets. In contrast, the study of nutrition has been studied very well in the modern era. Athletes know what they should and shouldn’t eat. Many athletes these days eat high-protein, whole foods. The way that muscles grow has been extensively researched, and scientists have concluded that eating high-protein foods is what contributes to muscle growth. Athletes have realized that having stronger muscles will contribute to better performance in whichever sport they are competing in. This shows the biggest disparity between the diets of ancient and modern athletes. The ancient Greek athletes probably were not able to maximize the amount of growth they could have had because of their lack of knowledge of nutrition.
