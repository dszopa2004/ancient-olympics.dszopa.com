The Significance of Athleticism in ancient Greece

Sports and athletics were of utmost importance to many of the ancient Greek people. While to some ancient Greek people, sports may not have been as important, to others it was just as important as glory. On some occasions, formal games were held at events: “honor funerals, celebrate military victories, mark the foundations of cities, and to pay homage to gods and heroes” (Poliakoff). In the poem, the Odyssey, having a proper funeral was shown to be important to the ancient Greeks. When Odysseus visited the underworld, one of his dead shipmates requested a proper burial. After their meeting, Odysseus did exactly what was requested of him. This shows just how important burials must have been for the ancient Greek people. For these athletic games to be held at funerals shows just how big of a role the athletic games must have been in their lives. Another example of how important athleticism is can be found in the military. For instance, in ancient Sparta, whenever the Spartan soldiers were low in confidence, their king gave them an opportunity to show their athleticism: “When the Spartan king Agesilaus wanted to hearten his troops, he stripped and exhibited naked captured soldiers of the enemy Persian forces in order to show how unathletic they were in comparison to Greek men and how easily they would fall in battle” (Poliakoff). This helps illustrate just how important athleticism was to some people in ancient Greece. Not only does this show how important athleticism was to the morale of the soldiers, but it also shows how they were able to use this to gain dominance over their opponents. This can still be held somewhat true in the modern world. Modern countries can sometimes demonstrate the athleticism of their militaries through physical tests. Some common tests include push-ups, pull-ups, sit-ups, and long-distance runs. Other than physical tests, the modern world does not usually use athleticism to demonstrate power. Some may argue that military weapon testing can be used to demonstrate power, but that does not show the capabilities of the physical abilities of a country's power.
