Who Could Participate in the Ancient Olympics?

Men were not the only people who could compete in the ancient Olympics. Women were able to participate in these events as well: “The games young, unmarried women competed in were celebrated for Hera” (Hemingway). This shows that women even had a god that they sponsored during their Olympics. There is even literature that depicts women participating in sports: “Of all the well-known female characters in the Iliad and the Odyssey only Nausicaa, the young virgin daughter of King Alcinous and Queen Arete, indulged in playful games and sport-like activities” (Spears). One important thing to note is that “the language of the poem is explicit in its use of words to distinguish play from sport” (Spears). While the language in the poem does not explicitly state that the woman is participating in sports, she is still depicted as playing in sport-like activities.

Another way that women are depicted in athletics is through art. According to Betty Spears, there are vases and statuettes that “show a young girl running” (Spears). This is very interesting because normally only men are depicted to be doing some sort of athletic activity such as hunting. As a result, the art is a very strong hint that points towards the ability of women to participate in the ancient Olympics. It is also very surprising that young, unmarried women were allowed to compete in these events. Up until recent history, women were not seen as athletes: “In the United States neither intercollegiate championships nor athletic scholarships for women existed in 1966” (Spears). This illustrates how the ancient Greek society can be seen as progressive. Although women in ancient Greece were generally seen as property, they were still offered an opportunity to be seen as an athlete, according to the ancient Greek definition of an athlete. However, this is very different from how modern women are able to participate in the Olympics. In the modern era, women, married or unmarried, are able to compete in any event that they may qualify for in the modern Olympics.
